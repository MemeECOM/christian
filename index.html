How We Find Winning Products

The 6-Step System

Every product must pass ALL 6 tests before we sell it. Think of it like a checklist - if any step fails, we don't sell the product.

1. Good Market Potential

The market should have some competition but not too much. Sweet spot: 5-15 other sellers actively advertising the same product.

Example: If only 2 people are selling it = market too small. If 25+ people are selling it = too crowded, hard to make money.

2. Evergreen (Long-lasting)

Product should sell well for at least 12 months, not just a short trend. Seasonal products are OK if they come back every year.

Example: Phone cases = evergreen. Fidget spinners = short trend (bad). Christmas decorations = seasonal but recurring (good).

3. Solves a Real Problem

People should buy it because they need it, not just because it looks cool. It must fix something annoying in their life.

Example: Posture corrector = solves back pain. Decorative lamp = just looks nice (harder to sell).

4. Good Quality

Product reviews should average 4.2 stars or higher. We need to check what customers complain about in bad reviews.

Example: Read 1-star reviews on Amazon. If everyone says "broke after 1 week" = quality problem.

5. Good Profit Margins

Simple rule: Supplier price × 3 = Our selling price. This gives us 66% profit margin minimum.

Example: Supplier charges $10 → We sell for $30. After ads and fees, we keep $15+ profit.

6. Upsell Opportunities

Can we sell extra items with the main product? Different colors, sizes, or accessories increase our profit per customer.

Example: Selling a lamp? Also offer: different colors, mounting kit, extra bulbs, carrying case.

What's Missing From Your Research

Problem #1: No Clickable Links
You showed screenshots but we can't click to verify your information. We need to be able to check your sources ourselves.
How to Fix This:
Create a Google Sheets document with clickable links to:
  • Competitor websites you analyzed
  • Supplier profiles on Alibaba
  • Facebook/TikTok ads you found
  • Product pages you studied

Tools to use:

Google Sheets Google Docs
Problem #2: Only One Supplier
You found one supplier with no reviews. This is risky - what if they disappear or have problems? We need backup options.
How to Fix This:
Find at least 3 suppliers by:
  • Upload product image to reverse search
  • Look for suppliers with 100+ sales
  • Check they have 4.5+ star ratings
  • Compare prices and shipping times

Where to search:

1688.com Alibaba.com
Problem #3: No Competitor Ad Analysis
You didn't check what ads competitors are running. We need to see what's working before we spend money on our own ads.
How to Fix This:
Research competitor ads:
  • Find 3-5 successful ads for similar products
  • Note what message/hook they use
  • See which platforms they advertise on most
  • Count how many sellers are actively advertising

Free tools to use:

Facebook Ad Library TikTok Creative Center
Problem #4: No Negative Review Analysis
You only looked at positive aspects. We need to know what problems customers have so we can prepare solutions.
How to Fix This:
Study bad reviews systematically:
  • Read 1-2 star reviews on Amazon and AliExpress
  • List the 3 most common complaints
  • Calculate what % of customers have each problem
  • Prepare answers for these issues on our product page

Where to check:

Amazon Reviews AliExpress Reviews
Problem #5: No Personal Assessment
You didn't give your honest opinion about the product. We value your perspective - would you actually buy this yourself?
How to Fix This:
Give your honest evaluation:
  • Rate each of our 6 criteria from 1-10
  • Explain your reasoning for each score
  • Note any concerns or red flags
  • Would you personally buy this product?
Problem #6: No Profit Calculation
You didn't check if we can actually make money selling this. Profit calculation is essential before choosing any product.
How to Fix This:
Calculate real profitability:
  • Supplier cost + shipping + taxes = Total cost
  • Our selling price - Total cost = Gross profit
  • Gross profit should be at least 60% of selling price
  • Factor in advertising costs (usually 20-30% of revenue)
